Famous Mechanical Engineers:

Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian American, mechanical and electrical engineer whose inventions include Tesla coil, AC electricity, & discovered rotating magnetic field. AC-predominantly used today, Tesla coil used in radio technology & in early radio transmission antennas. His mother invented small household appliances, which inspired him to work in this field. Pioneer of radar technology, x-ray tech, remote control & rotating magnetic field-basis of AC machinery Designed one of the first hydroelectrical power plants in the US and worked with Thomas Edison.

Thomas Edison

Thomas Edison was an American inventor who invented the first iridescent lightbulb. Edison helped build the U.S. during the Industrial Revolution. He also experimented with telegraph technology, and became familiar with electrical science Edison's inventions include the telegraph, phonograph, and other technologies

Gustaf de Laval

Gustaf de Laval was a Swedish engineer and inventor who introduced the concept of an impulse steam turbine. De Laval developed a nozzle to increase the steam jet to supersonic speed, called de Laval nozzle used today in rocket engine nozzles. He also developed the first centrifugal milk-cream separator and early milking machines.
