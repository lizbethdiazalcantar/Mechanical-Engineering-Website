Build a Robot!

In this activity we will learn about heat transfer, a theory that many Mechanical Engineers use to solve real world problems. Heat transfer is the study of how thermal energy transfers from one location to another via a medium and a method. Mediums can be solids, liquids, gasses and vacuums. The 3 methods are Conduction, Convection and Radiation.

How to Play:

  1. Choose your components
    1. Build your Robot! Choose the version of components you'd like but make sure to account for the heat generated by those components. There's only so much heat the circuit board can dissipate without overheating and damaging the components. refer to the chart for limits and component's specs like k or thermal conductivity. Use the conduction equation to make sure the components you chose won't cause the board to overheat. The board can only hold 6 Watts.
  2. Once your have chosen your components, assemble the circuit board
    1. Do not put components too close. Heat can be additive. Do not burn the circuit board! Be sure to enter your name before you submit to merge your results! You only get 5 tries to test if your components will work.
